# Michelin

**Type:** Enterprise (multinational corporation, tyre and mobility)  
**Relevance:** AIOps early adopter; enterprise case study for bottom-up grassroots AIOps initiative within a global MCP governance framework

---

## AIOps Initiative

[[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Matthew Liu]], a China-based IT architect at Michelin, initiated an AIOps exploration as a local initiative within the global **Game Changer** AI programme. Key characteristics:

- **Platform:** [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Dify]] (low-code AI app builder) on AliCloud, connected to global [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/concepts/Model Context Protocol|MCP]] connectors
- **Governance model:** Regional pilot under global IT governance — explicitly *not* a China-only island
- **Approval scope:** Architectural, security, and data-handling approval obtained for the *MCP-based AIOps pattern* (not a specific product stack)
- **Current status (as of 2025-12-17):** In exploration phase — platform and governance in place, flagship use cases being defined with ops head

### Stakeholder Landscape

| Stakeholder | Concern |
|-------------|---------|
| IT managers | "What's the concrete value right now?" |
| Ops teams | "Will 30% time savings mean 30% fewer headcount?" |
| Architects/Governance | "We need architecture before scope is finalised" |
| Global IT | "Are you aligned or building your own island?" |

### Key Decisions

- Used vendor contract renewals as a channel (A8) to find politically safe, measurable flagship use cases
- Adopted [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/concepts/AIOps Exploration Charter]] framing — learning-first, not KPI-punishment — to unlock management sponsorship
- Leveraged [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Dify]] + ServiceNow MCP to build working AIOps demos in hours, which was critical for stakeholder buy-in
