# Matthew Liu

**Role:** IT Architect, [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Michelin]] China IT  
**Affiliation:** [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Michelin]]

---

## Who He Is

Matthew Liu is an IT architect and operations practitioner at [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Michelin]]'s China IT organisation. He initiated and led an AIOps exploration using [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Dify]] as a low-code AI app builder on AliCloud, aligned with [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Michelin]]'s **Game Changer** global AI programme and MCP governance model.

His published retrospective is notable for being unusually candid about the *actual* sequence of events, stakeholder resistance, and mistakes — making it one of the few practitioner accounts of enterprise AIOps adoption from the grassroots level.

---

## Contributions

- Authored "Rolling Out AIOps Without a Grand Vision: What We Did, What We Would Change" (Michelin IT blog, 2025-12-17)
- Pioneered the use of [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Dify]] + global [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/concepts/Model Context Protocol|MCP]] connectors (ServiceNow) for rapid AIOps demo development at Michelin
- Developed the concept of two-channel flagship use case discovery: external (vendor contracts) and internal (ops process mining)
- Documented the importance of an **[[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/concepts/AIOps Exploration Charter]]** — a learning-first, time-boxed commitment before KPI-heavy funding requests
