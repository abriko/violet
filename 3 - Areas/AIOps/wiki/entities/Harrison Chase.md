# Harrison Chase

**Type:** Person
**Role:** CEO and co-founder of [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/LangChain]]

---

## What He Does

Harrison Chase leads [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/LangChain]], the open-source LLM application framework. He authored "How and when to build multi-agent systems" (June 2025), which reconciled the conflicting positions of [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Cognition]] and [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/entities/Anthropic]] on multi-agent architectures.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Read-vs-write distinction** for [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/concepts/Multi-Agent Architecture]]: read-heavy tasks (research) are well-suited to parallel multi-agent; write-heavy tasks (code) need single-threaded agents
- Endorsed [[3 - Areas/AIOps/wiki/concepts/Context Engineering]] as the #1 discipline for agent reliability
- Positioned LangGraph's "no hidden prompts" philosophy as the correct approach to context engineering — frameworks should not inject hidden prompt content
- Identified durable execution, observability, and evaluation as the three production challenges for multi-agent systems
